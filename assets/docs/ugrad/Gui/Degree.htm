Bachelor of Science in Computer Science (BSCS) as a Broad-Field Major

Students pursuing the Bachelor of Science in Computer Science (BSCS) as a broad-field major must complete the University core requirements and the Computer Science core. No minor is required for this degree.


University Core Requirements 43 hours

Complete the requirements 48 hour University Core Requirements. PHIL 2390 (Professional Ethics) must be taken to meet the philosophy requirement. Courses to meet the natural science requirement must be taken from Biology, Chemistry, or Physics. Note: the computer literacy and mathematics requirements (the remaining 5 hours of the general education requirements) can be met by required courses listed below.

Computer Science Core Courses 27 hours

CSCI 1380 Computer Science I
CSCI 1381 Foundations of Computer Science
CSCI 2330 C++ Programming
CSCI 2333 Computer Organization and Assembly Language
CSCI 2380 Computer Science II
CSCI 3333 Algorithms and Data Structures
CSCI 3334 Systems Programming
CSCI 3336 Organization of Programming Languages
CSCI 4390 Senior Project


Designated Electives 21 hours

Select 3 hours from the following: CSCI 2320, CSCI 2325, CIS 2308, CIS 2310, or any advanced CSCI course.

Select 18 hours of advanced CSCI courses, must include 6 hours selected from CSCI 3340, 4334, and 4335.

A maximum of 3 hours from CSCI 3300 and CSCI 3390 can by used to fulfill CSCI elective requirements.


Mathematics / Engineering 21 hours

MATH 1401 Calculus I
MATH 1402 Calculus II
MATH 2345 Elementary Linear Algebra
MATH 3373 Discrete Structures
ELEE 2130 Digital Systems Laboratory
ELEE 2330 Digital Systems Engineering I

Select 3 hours from the following:
MATH 3337 Applied Statistics I
MATH 4339 Probability and Statistics
ELEE 3340 Probability and Statistics for Electrical Engineers


Communications / English 6 hours

ENG 3333 Technical Report Writing
Select 3 hours from the following:
COMM 1303 Presentational Speaking
COMM 1313 Fundamentals of Speech Communication


Lab Science 4 hours

Any 4 hour laboratory science course from Biology, Chemistry, or Physics (this is in addition to the general education requirement, but need not be in the same subject as the 8 hours taken for that requirement).


Other Electives 9-12 hours

Students must select electives to complete a total of 51 advanced hours in their degree. Depending on the selection of electives, this will require from 9 to 12 hours of advanced electives.


Other Requirements

Students must complete all Computer Science Core Courses with a grade of "C" or better.


TOTAL 131-134 hours
